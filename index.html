Ragunathan Laboratory · Brandeis University

How chromatin encodes heritable memory

We study how patterns of histone modification are established, propagated through DNA replication, and inherited across cell divisions — resolving the mechanisms of epigenetic inheritance in S. pombe and beyond.

After Hasegawa Tōhaku, Pine Trees (松林図屏風, c. 1595).
Research

Questions that guide us

We use Schizosaccharomyces pombe as a primary model, integrating genetics, biochemistry, and single-molecule biophysics to resolve how chromatin states are established, maintained, and inherited — and we extend these questions into non-model fungi and mammalian cells.

Epigenetic Inheritance

Using inducible systems in fission yeast, we separate the establishment of H3K9 methylation from its maintenance, defining the molecular requirements for heritable heterochromatin and how HP1 proteins switch between these two modes.

Heterochromatin Dynamics

We track Swi6/HP1, Clr4, and associated remodelers at single-molecule resolution in living cells, measuring the binding kinetics and transient interactions through which fleeting molecular events sustain stable, long-lived silencing.

Epigenetic Adaptation

Cells can acquire heritable phenotypes by redistributing histone modifications without genetic change. We quantify the slow kinetics of this adaptation and trace the paths by which populations converge on stable epigenetic states.

Synthetic Epigenetic Circuits

By reconstituting minimal chromatin circuits, we test the design principles of epigenetic memory — mapping the conditions under which feedback yields bistability, oscillation, or stable inheritance.

Chromatin in Non-Model Fungi

We move beyond classic models to polyextremotolerant fungi such as Aureobasidium pullulans, asking how chromatin-based memory lets cells adapt to environmental stress and switch heritably between cellular states.

Methods

What we use, and what for

A quick tour of the techniques we combine across projects.

Genetics

Forward and reverse genetic screens in fission yeast to identify and order the factors that build heterochromatin.

Single-Molecule Imaging

Tracking individual silencing factors in living cells to measure binding kinetics and transient interactions.

Biochemical Reconstitution

Rebuilding chromatin-modifying complexes and nucleosome arrays in vitro to dissect enzymatic mechanisms.

ChIP-seq & RNA-seq

Genome-wide maps of histone modifications and transcription, followed over time during inheritance and adaptation.

Synthetic Biology

Minimal chromatin circuits built from defined parts to test the design principles of epigenetic memory.

Mathematical Modeling

Quantitative models of epigenetic feedback and single-cell lineages linking molecules to populations.

Opportunities

Join us

We look for curious scientists drawn towards hard mechanistic questions. Experience in yeast is not required — we value range of perspective and the willingness to learn a new system.

We are actively recruiting. When you write, please put [Ragunathan Lab — Position] in your email subject line so your message reaches us.

Postdoctoral Scholars

For candidates with interests in genetics, biochemistry, biophysics, or quantitative biology in any model system.

Include in your email
  • A brief cover letter describing your interests
  • Your CV, including a publication list
  • A one-page research statement
  • Names and contact details of three references

Graduate Students

Apply through the Molecular & Cell Biology (MCB)/Biochemistry & Biophysics graduate program at Brandeis. We welcome rotation students from all Brandeis associated graduate programs.

Before you rotate
  • Email to arrange a conversation about projects
  • Tell us which research thrust interests you and why
  • Mention any prior research experience

Undergraduates

We host undergraduates for research in a range of capacities throughout the year.

Include in your email
  • A short statement of interest and career goals
  • Your area of interest (genetics, biochemistry, etc.)
  • Your availability and an unofficial transcript
